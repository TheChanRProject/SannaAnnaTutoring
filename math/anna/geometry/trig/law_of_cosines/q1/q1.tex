\begin{center}
To find b, we must use the law of cosines.
\begin{align}
  b^2 = 16^2 + 11^2 + 2(16*11*cos(33^{\circ})) \\
  b^2 = 256 + 121 + 2(172cos(33)) \\
  b = \sqrt{377 + 344cos(33)} \approx 25.9
\end{align}
